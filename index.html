What's happening to Coastal City Marea

Coastal City Marea sits at the mouth of the Verea River delta. Over the past decade, upstream deforestation has accelerated runoff, while rising sea levels have reduced the delta's natural drainage capacity by an estimated 38%.

Three consecutive drought years followed by extreme precipitation events have overwhelmed aging stormwater infrastructure. The city's Canal District — home to 47,000 residents and its historic market — sits 1.2 metres below projected flood peaks.

Climate models give a 73% probability of a Category 2 flood event in the coming storm season. Without intervention, estimated economic damage exceeds €180 million and habitat loss across the delta wetlands would eliminate nesting grounds for 12 protected bird species.

Key threat factors: accelerated upstream runoff · degraded wetland buffers · undersized drainage channels built for 1970s rainfall patterns · a community that has lost trust in government promises after two failed projects
Your mission
What you and your team must do

You are the newly appointed Emergency Flood Coordinator for the Marea Metropolitan Authority. You have 60 days and a €2.4 million emergency budget to design and execute a flood response strategy before storm season begins.

Your mandate is not just engineering — you must balance community trust, limited resources, ecological protection, and political pressure. Not every intervention is affordable, not every stakeholder agrees, and some data you receive is incomplete or contradictory.
